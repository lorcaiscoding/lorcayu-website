The Marvelous Clouds (2024)

exhibited at 2024 Coney Island Maker Faire
3D-printed television, microcontroller, LED matrix panel, frosted acrylic, tactile push buttons

This project is a sky TV. It consists of two buttons and a screen. The screen is made up of an LED matrix panel and a piece of frosted acrylic. By calling an API, cloud-cover data from Brooklyn is retrieved and refreshed every minute. The percentage of cloud cover is displayed as white light on the LED panel, while the remaining part is shown as blue light. This creates a real-time, site-specific sky simulator.

Through the simulation, the dynamic processes of nature are transformed into a minimalistic data visualization. This is not merely a screen displaying cloud coverage; it interprets the changing sky as a statistical chart — with the distribution of blue and white representing the binary concept of "yes" and "no." But the true absurdity lies in the fact that this seemingly precise binary judgment is, in reality, entirely meaningless. Although the "yes" and "no" buttons suggest the audience's choice, pressing them doesn't change anything. The distinction between "yes" and "no" is determined entirely by the color of the sky rather than any rational standard. This design serves as a critique of modern methods of statistical judgment, exposing how contemporary society, in its pursuit of control and simplification, often overlooks the complexity and uncontrollability of nature.

In this way, viewers are invited to reflect on the inherent meaninglessness of rational logic and to reconsider the relationship between the natural and the artificial, between data and reality. By using the simplest artificial light to mimic natural light within constructed spaces, the project aims to capture the tension between the interior and exterior, blurring the boundaries between what is built and what exists beyond. Moments are created where the sky becomes not just a backdrop but an active participant in the dialogue between space, art, and the human experience.
