God Is a Woman Sitting in the Bath (2024)

projector, 3D-printed bathtube, real-time tide data
Soft Mountains out of water
White coasts of soap scum
The tides of standing
To wash her belly
To wash underneath her breasts
Thank you god for this time of plenty
Before you are clean
And get out of the universe
Leaving the water still
And dark

This project uses real-time tide data from The Battery, NY (tide station at the southern tip of Manhattan) to create a projection that portrays the posture of a woman bathing. The woman's posture shifts based on the tide: at the lowest tide, she leisurely stretches her arms and legs out of the bathtub, while at the highest tide, she submerges her body entirely except for her head. The fictional rise and fall of water levels within the bathtub, driven by her posture changes, serve as a metaphor for the ebb and flow of tides.
