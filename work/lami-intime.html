L'ami intime (2024)

exhibited at the 2024 Coney Island Maker Faire
wood, clear resin 3D print, scented candle

The inspiration for this project comes from René Magritte's painting L'ami intime. I love how he uses the metaphor of a baguette and a wine glass to interpret friendship, as it reminds me of my undergraduate days in Beijing (yes, a difficult time). Each weekend, I would randomly go with one of my friends to a different exotic restaurant in the embassy district. It was the only opportunity to break free from our East Asian daily routine, which was entirely centered around rice. We ate baguettes, German roasted pork knuckles, Basbousa, and drank red wine like real adults. I'm not someone who enjoys large crowds, so I rarely have a large group of friends — most of my friendships are one-on-one. This suddenly made me realize that if you adjust the positions of the baguette and wine glass in the painting, they can form a small table for two.

I use the combination of shadows and movement to convey this dual meaning. Since suspended objects better reflect the surrealism in Magritte's paintings, I think automata would be a good medium for this expression. I set two extreme points of motion for the relative positions of the baguette and wine glass: one replicates where they are in Magritte's painting, while the other allows their shadows to form the outline of a table for two on the wall behind. They move back and forth between these two states over time. An apple-scented candle is used as the light source to add a sensory dimension to the performance, because the scent always reminds me of the coldest days of winter when my friend and I would eat apple pancakes together in the Russian restaurant.

So the whole light performance goes like this: when the lighter is first lit, it's like starting a fire for the stove, signifying the beginning of dinner. As the candlelight flickers and the fragrance spreads, the physical and shadow symbols alternate through mechanical motion. When the candle finally burns out, the beautiful dinner comes to an end, as all good things must come to a close.

A table for two can symbolize a date, a catch-up, or a reunion; all these experiences and conversations reflect our relationships and who we are.
